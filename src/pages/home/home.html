INDIAN CEO'S

Sundar Pichai

It is not every day that one of the world's largest technology companies announces a new CEO.

So when Google announced that Sundar Pichai was taking the reins on Monday, his promotion gained thousands of column inches worldwide - not least of all in his native India.


Satya Nadella

Satya Nadella, who hails from the Hyderabad, Telangana, India. Now is the current chief executive officer of Microsoft.


Shantanu Narayen

Mr Narayen, from Hyderabad, has run the software firm since December 2007. He began his career with Apple.


Indra Nooyi

Named the third most powerful woman in business by Fortune magazine last year, Ms Nooyi was, like Sundar Pichai, born in Chennai.


Ajay Banga

After working for Nestle, then PepsiCo, Mr Banga - who is from Pune - took over as CEO of the credit card company in July 2010. He began his career with Nestle in 1981.


Nikesh Arora

Nikesh Arora, one of Google’s most high profile employees quit last year to join as vice chairman of SoftBank Corp and CEO of SoftBank Internet and Media Inc.


Ivan Menezes

Mr Menezes, from Pune, is another IIM graduate. He took over as head of the British drinks giant Diageo in July 2013.


Piyush Gupta

CEO at DBS Group Holdings and DBS Bank, Piyush Gupta, is an alumnus of IIM Ahmedabad.


Rajeev Vasudeva

Rajeev Vasudeva is CEO of Zurich-based executive search firm Egon Zehnder. Mr.Vasudeva joined Egon Zehnder in 1995 when his consultancy Sachar Vasudeva & Associates merged with Egon Zehnder.


Sanjay Kumar Jha

Sanjay Kumar Jha who hails from Bihar is now the CEO of Global Foundries, the world’s first full-service semiconductor foundry. He was appointed CEO of GLOBALFOUNDRIES in January 2014.


Francisco D’Souza

Indian-origin Francisco D’Souza is the chief executive officer of Cognizant. He was appointed CEO in January 2007 and is ranked among the youngest chief executive officers in the software services sector.
